STEFANUS HENRY GUNAWAN

Overview

In May 2025, I attended a Product Design Bootcamp to deepen my knowledge in the field. During the program, I worked on a project to develop a real-life applicable app together with two teammates. I was also responsible for leading the team and managing the workload throughout the bootcamp.

Problem Statement

Children often worry about their elderly parents, especially when they live alone. However, many older adults dislike being disturbed, are reluctant to use complicated apps, or don’t want to feel like they are being watched.

Objective

We need a passive system that allows families, especially children to receive daily updates about their parents’ condition while still maintaining their comfort and sense of independence.

We plan our research to define what kind of data we will collect and how we will gather it.

User Persona

To ensure the app effectively meets the needs of both children and elderly parents, we created two distinct user personas that reflect the app’s core purpose and align with its target market.

Pain Point

Based on interviews with friends and family, we identified several key concerns regarding family tracking apps. Users expressed worries about privacy, the complexity of app interfaces for elderly users, and the potential for feeling monitored or losing independence.

Affinity Diagram

We synthesized the collected problems into broader categories using the Affinity Diagram method, enabling us to identify key themes and prioritize areas for improvement.

How Do We Solve The Problems?

We brainstormed solutions as a team to address the problems identified in the Affinity Diagram. Through collaborative discussion, we developed several practical ideas, including:

  • Simplifying the app interface for elderly users with large buttons and clear instructions.
  • Implementing passive check-in features that update family members without requiring frequent user interaction.
  • Protecting user privacy by designing a closed tracking system that restricts data sharing exclusively within the family group.
  • Offering onboarding guidance for both children and parents to build trust and confidence in using the app.

Prototype - Wireframe

We started working on prototypes to get a better look at our solution and to lay down all the foundation of what our app is going to look like.

Style Guide

Once the low-fidelity prototype was finalized, we developed a comprehensive style guide to establish the app’s visual identity. This included careful selection of typography, color palette, and overall design language to ensure consistency and enhance user experience. Blue was chosen as the primary color to evoke a sense of safety and trustworthiness, aligning with the app’s core values and target audience.

We also created a set of assets for the final design, including custom icons, clear field text, and accessible buttons. These elements were designed to enhance usability and maintain a cohesive visual style throughout the app, ensuring that both elderly users and their families can navigate the interface with ease and confidence.

First Design Iteration

Usability Testing

After completing our first iteration of the MataKeluarga app, we conducted usability testing with five relevant users. Each participant was asked to use the app and share their experiences, thought processes, and opinions on the design. Through these interviews, we identified several key insights:

  • Users appreciated the app’s simplicity and clear navigation, especially the large buttons and straightforward instructions.
  • During initial login, some users confused the login button with the signup button. This indicates a need for different hierarchy between login and signup actions.
  • Some elderly users couldn't find the 'Location History' feature and they looked slightly confused due to lack of visual guidance.
  • Several users were confused about whether they should scan a QR code or display their own QR code during the pairing process. This indicates the need for clearer instructions and visual cues to help users easily navigate between scanning and showing their QR code.
  • Privacy controls were well received, but users wanted more flexibility in choosing what information to share.
  • Overall, the design was positively received, but further refinements were needed to enhance accessibility and comfort for elderly users.

After reviewing user feedback, we introduced new designs for the MataKeluarga app to enhance usability and overall user experience.

By swapping the positions of the “Sign In” and “Register” buttons, new users will feel more comfortable when registering, considering there are more users registering than signing in.

When opening the “Location” tab, the initial screen immediately displays the location history of family members, providing users with quick access to recent movements and ensuring ease of monitoring.

To improve clarity during mobile pairing, the screen now features two distinct tabs: “Scan QR” and “My QR”. This separation helps users easily understand the difference between scanning a family member’s QR code to pair devices, and displaying their own QR code for others to scan. Clear visual cues and instructions are provided on each tab to guide users through the pairing process with confidence.
